My Favourite People

K.Pawan Kalyan

Pawan Kalyan (born Konidela Kalyan Kumar on 2 September 1971) is a prominent Indian actor, politician, martial artist, and philanthropist primarily associated with Telugu cinema. He is the younger brother of famous actor and politician Chiranjeevi. Kalyan made his acting debut in 1996 with the film Akkada Ammayi Ikkada Abbayi and quickly rose to fame with hits like Tholi Prema, Thammudu, Badri, and Kushi. He is known for his unique style and has a massive, loyal fanbase often described as a "cult following." He holds a black belt in Karate and was awarded the title "Pawan" by the Isshin-ryū Karate Association after a public martial arts demonstration. In addition to his film career, Pawan Kalyan is a significant political figure. He founded the Janasena Party in 2014 and has been active in Andhra Pradesh politics. In the 2024 elections, he played a crucial role in an alliance with the TDP and BJP, leading to a landslide victory. He was elected as an MLA from the Pithapuram constituency and appointed as the 11th Deputy Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh in June 2024. He also holds the portfolios of Panchayat Raj, Rural Development & Rural Water Supply, Environment, Forests, Science & Technology.

Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli is one of the most successful and celebrated cricketers in the world, known for his exceptional batting skills and leadership. He has scored over 27,500 international runs across all formats, making him the third highest run-scorer in international cricket history, behind only Kumar Sangakkara and Sachin Tendulkar. Kohli holds numerous records, including being the fastest to reach multiple ODI milestones such as 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 13,000 runs. He has scored the most ODI centuries (50) and the most centuries while chasing (27). In World Cups, he holds the record for the most runs in a single edition, with 765, and was the first player to score over 700 runs in a tournament. Kohli is also the first cricketer to have played 300 ODIs, 100 Tests, and 100 T20 internationals, showcasing his versatility across formats. In T20 internationals, he boasts the highest career batting average (52.73) and is the leading run-scorer with over 4,000 runs. In the Indian Premier League (IPL), he is the highest run-scorer overall, with 7,263 runs, and holds records for most fours and hundreds. As a captain, Kohli led India to major ICC trophies, including the 2011 ODI World Cup, the 2013 and 2025 ICC Champions Trophy, and the 2024 T20 World Cup. His outstanding performances have earned him prestigious awards such as the Padma Shri in 2017, the Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award in 2018, and multiple ICC accolades including Cricketer of the Year and ODI Player of the Year. Known for his aggressive batting style, consistency, and ability to perform under pressure, especially while chasing, Virat Kohli has firmly established himself as one of the greatest batsmen and captains in cricket history.

Y. S. Jagan Mohan Reddy

Yeduguri Sandinti Jagan Mohan Reddy, widely known as Y. S. Jagan Mohan Reddy, is an Indian politician and entrepreneur born on December 21, 1972, in Pulivendula, Andhra Pradesh. He is the son of former Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister Y. S. Rajasekhara Reddy. Jagan began his career in business before entering politics, initially campaigning for the Indian National Congress in 2004. In 2009, he was elected as the Member of Parliament from Kadapa. After the sudden death of his father in a helicopter crash in 2009, Jagan founded the YSR Congress Party (YSRCP) in 2011, embarking on a massive padayatra to connect with the people across Andhra Pradesh. His political journey was marked by both rapid ascent and legal challenges, including a high-profile arrest in 2012 on embezzlement charges. Despite setbacks, Jagan led the YSRCP to a historic victory in the 2019 Andhra Pradesh Assembly elections, winning 151 out of 175 seats and becoming the state’s Chief Minister. His tenure was characterized by a focus on welfare schemes like Jagananna Amma Vodi and Navaratnalu, as well as the controversial proposal to establish three capitals for the state. However, in the 2024 elections, his party suffered a significant defeat, dropping to just 11 seats, after which he resigned as Chief Minister. Jagan remains a prominent figure in Andhra Pradesh politics and continues to serve as the MLA for Pulivendula.

Ratan Naval Tata

Ratan Tata (1937–2024) was a legendary Indian industrialist and philanthropist who served as the chairman of Tata Sons and the Tata Group from 1991 to 2012, with an additional stint as interim chairman from 2016 to 2017. Born into the prominent Tata family, he began his career in 1961 working at the grassroots level on the shop floor of Tata Steel. Taking over from his uncle JRD Tata in 1991, Ratan Tata transformed the Tata Group from a largely India-focused conglomerate into a global powerhouse, expanding its annual revenue from about $5 billion to nearly $100 billion. Under his visionary leadership, the group made landmark acquisitions such as Tetley Tea, Corus Steel, Jaguar Land Rover, and Daewoo’s truck manufacturing unit, significantly boosting its international presence. He also spearheaded the launch of iconic products like the Tata Indica, India’s first indigenously designed passenger car, and the Tata Nano, famously marketed as the world’s most affordable car. Ratan Tata was committed to social welfare, directing a large portion of the company’s profits toward charitable trusts focused on education, healthcare, and rural development. His leadership style emphasized innovation, consolidation, and grooming young talent, and he implemented key reforms such as setting retirement ages to rejuvenate the group’s management. For his contributions, he was honored with India’s Padma Bhushan in 2000 and the Padma Vibhushan in 2008. Even after retirement, he remained influential as the head of Tata Trusts and continued to invest in startups and serve on various global corporate boards. Ratan Tata’s legacy is that of a transformative leader who globalized Indian industry while maintaining a strong commitment to philanthropy and social responsibility.

Dr. P. Satish Kumar

Dr. Satish Kumar is the founder and senior pastor of Calvary Temple, a prominent megachurch located in Hyderabad, Andhra Pradesh. Born in 1971 into a poor family, Satish Kumar faced a troubled childhood and adolescence, struggling with negative influences before experiencing a profound personal transformation after a spiritual encounter with Jesus Christ. Inspired by this change, he dedicated his life to Christian service, starting with youth fellowship activities and eventually founding Calvary Temple in 2005 with just 25 members. Under his visionary leadership, the church experienced rapid growth, reaching nearly 200,000 members by 2018 and becoming known for its massive sanctuary, which was famously constructed in just 52 days a feat often compared to the biblical story of Nehemiah. Calvary Temple is recognized for its large-scale humanitarian efforts, such as distributing 800 tons of food to the poor during the 2020 COVID-19 lockdown, reaching tens of thousands of families across Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. Dr. Satish Kumar is respected not only for his dynamic preaching and leadership but also for his commitment to service, inclusivity, and promoting values of love, hope, and redemption. He has received several honors, including the Doctor of Divinity award from Dallas Baptist University and the Mother Teresa Award for his humanitarian work. Calvary Temple operates as a non-denominational, evangelical church and is considered a trailblazer in the Indian Christian community, blending global megachurch trends with local cultural values.
